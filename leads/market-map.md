### Weekly scan — 2026-06-05
Three trends worth knowing:
1. **Seed-stage AI-ed valuations are resetting upward.** Vimi ($12M seed), Pensive ($6.8M seed), and Gizmo (seed → $22M Series A) all priced richly in early 2026 — investors are paying up at seed again, but rounds are still small enough that none can staff a dedicated curriculum-architecture function. ([newmarketpitch](https://newmarketpitch.com/blogs/news/ai-education-top-startups-fundraising))
2. **Assessment/grading is becoming the funded wedge into personalized learning.** Historically grading + assessment took only ~16.7% of AI-ed capital; now Pensive and Mathew (handwritten-task AI grading) are using it as a beachhead toward "personalized support." That's a direct opening for misconception-as-substrate + DoK/QC work. ([Pensive](https://www.pensive.com/schools/blog/seed-funding/))
3. **"Curriculum-aligned" personalized content generation is going international** (Mathew → LatAm, Vimi → school-curriculum-adaptive). "Aligned to curriculum" is now a table-stakes marketing claim — which is exactly where weak sequencing and thin QC hide once content multiplies per market. ([theaiinsider](https://theaiinsider.tech/2026/06/01/mathew-raises-e1-2m-seed-round-to-expand-ai-education-platform-across-latin-america/))

Held back to vet: **Gizmo** (London; notes → AI flashcards/quizzes, 13M users, [$22M Series A](https://tech.eu/2026/04/15/gizmo-secures-22m-series-a-to-advance-ai-powered-education/)) — verified, but B2C engagement/gamification focus and larger scale make it a weaker fit for the knowledge-architecture lens and a harder solo entry. Revisit if they push into structured course content.
